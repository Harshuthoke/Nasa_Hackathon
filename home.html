THE HISTORY

EMIT was developed at JPL and built by Ball Aerospace & Technologies. The instrument was launched on July 14, 2022, on a SpaceX Falcon 9 rocket from Cape Canaveral Space Force Station in Florida. EMIT was attached to the International Space Station (ISS) on July 17, 2022. EMIT began collecting science data on August 2, 2022. The instrument is expected to operate for at least five years.

But how does it work?

EMIT uses special technology to look at how sunlight bounces off the ground. This sunlight has different colors, and these colors help scientists figure out what kind of rocks and minerals are on the ground. EMIT can look at big areas on the Earth's surface all at once, and it can even see small details. EMIT's information helps scientists make maps that show where dusty areas are. These maps are important because they help us understand how dust moves in the air and how it can change the Earth's climate.
